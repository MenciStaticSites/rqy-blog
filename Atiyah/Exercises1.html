Proof. If xn=0 then (1+x)(1−x+x2−x3+...+(−1)n−1xn−1)=1, so 1+x is a unit. If a is a unit and x is a nilpotent, then a−1x is a nilpotent, hence a+x=a(1+a−1x) is a unit.

Exercise 4. In the ring A[x], the Jacobson radical is equal to nilradical.

Proof. Let f∈A[x] belong to Jacobson radical, then for all g∈A[x], 1−fg is a unit. In particular, 1−xf is a unit, hence any coefficient of f is nilpotent, and f is nilpotent, i.e. f belongs to nilradical of A[x].
